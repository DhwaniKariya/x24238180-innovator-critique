Historical fact card

Castro de Barona, in Porto do Son (Galicia), is an Iron Age coastal settlement occupied from roughly the first millennium BCE into the Roman era; in the 16th century, the wider Ria de Muros-Noia coastline faced repeated privateer incursions, which helped shape local watch practices and maritime defense culture still referenced in regional heritage records today.

Ingredient card

This batch uses Pemento de Herbón (Padrón pepper) cold extract from a small family farm near Padrón, Galicia. The peppers are dried and steeped in a low-temperature infusion before blending, adding a faint delayed warmth after the watermelon note.
